About Stage Fright

Stage Fright started as just a couple of friends making music with no real idea of where it would go. Now however, Stage Fright has become one of the most renound rock bands of the 21st century. With more grammies than you can count, hundreds of thousands of monthly listeners across all platforms, and three albums under their belt, they could only be described as the champions of the modern rock world.

Their goal as a band is to convey the feeling of various fears through music. they started with their debut album On a Ledge was made to convey the classic fear of heights. This album was Stage Fright's "adjustment period" as they said, where they were figuring out their sound, and experimenting with different areas of music. This album was more "metal-heavy" as described by critics, as they were still trying to figure out what they wanted to sound like.

They then came out with the sleeper hit Painted Face, which really put Stage Fright in the public eye. This album was focused on the fear of clowns, which was displayed with their experimental use of clown horns, circus music samples, and more that transported the listener into a terrifying yet thrilling experience. Painted Face, as of 1/10/25, has over 960,000 plays in total. This album gave Stage Fright the boost they needed to make themselves known in the modern rock world.

With Stage Fright's most recent album Gone But Not Forgotten, They truly sky-rocketed to the top of the rock scene. This album has a slighly deeper meaning and fear behind it, one that has cause much dismay to human kind since the dawn of our existence, that fear was being forgotten. GBNF (Gone But Not Forgotten) has been said to be Stage Fright's most experimental album. Their unique use of reverse sound effects, classical instruments, and haunting vocal sound effects, GBNF has been almost collectively agreed to be Stage Fright's most in-depth album. This album alone won Stage Fright 3 grammies, and over 900,000 mothly listeners. Now us Stage Fright Fenatics can only wait for Stage Fright's newest release and see what they have in store.
